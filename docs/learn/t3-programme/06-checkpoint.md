# Checkpoint — Programme readiness review

> **Tier 3 / Lesson 6 — 60 minutes.**

## What you should now own

By the end of T3 you should have, for *each of the 10 pillars*:

1. A discovery profile (one page).
2. A signed plan / policy / SOP (the Produce phase artefact).
3. Two leading + two lagging KPIs with baselines.
4. A named owner.

Plus, transversally:

- One executed TTX with a written AAR.
- One pillar-by-pillar dashboard showing KPI status.
- A risk register with at least 20 entries (use [`risk-management/risk-register.json`](#risk/register) as the structural model).
- A Steering Committee charter (Pillar 10 — IT/OT Teamwork) with named members.

## The Board pack test

Imagine your CEO asks: "we've been investing in OT security for 12 months. Show me what we have."

Your pack should answer, in order:

1. **What we have** — the 10 pillar artefacts, signed and dated.
2. **How we know it works** — the KPIs, with baselines and trends.
3. **What we tested** — the TTX AAR, with closed action items.
4. **What we owe the regulator** — the audit-package mapping (each artefact tagged to its NACSA section).
5. **What we don't have** — the residual risks in the register, with treatment plans and owners.

If you can produce that pack in 30 minutes from existing artefacts, T3 is done.

## Self-grading rubric

| | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Pillar coverage | All 10 pillars have at least the discovery + signed plan | Some pillars are blank or "to be done" |
| KPIs | Each pillar has 2L + 2L KPIs with a baseline | KPIs without baselines |
| TTX | One executed exercise with closed AAR action items | TTX done but no AAR, or AAR with stale action items |
| Risk register | 20+ entries, each with owner + treatment | Register exists but populated by IT alone |
| Steering Committee | Charter signed, met at least quarterly | Charter exists but no minutes |

If 4 of 5 pass, you're ready for T4. If fewer, finish the missing pillars before progressing.

## What you can now do

- Stand up the full programme from scratch in 12 months and survive a NACSA inspection.
- Execute and document a TTX.
- Defend a residual risk acceptance to the Board.

## What T4 adds

- The auditor's perspective: how *they* will assess what you've built.
- Evidence-gathering at speed.
- Finding-writing that maps to SR + NACSA section.
